AUSTRALIAN RESUME FORMAT SPECIFICATION

This file tells the AI how to structure and format the resume output.
Read every section before producing any content.

========================================================================
GENERAL RULES
========================================================================

Length:          2 to 3 pages. Do NOT limit to one page.
                 Australian recruiters expect detail. A one-page resume
                 reads as underbaked. Two pages is the standard for
                 entry-level candidates.

Language:        Australian English throughout. Use these spellings:
                 organisation (not organization)
                 specialised (not specialized)
                 analyse (not analyze)
                 optimise (not optimize)
                 behaviour (not behavior)
                 modelling (not modeling)
                 programme (only for non-software contexts; "program" for code)
                 favour, honour, colour, centre, defence

Formatting:      Plain and clean. No heavy design elements, no columns,
                 no colour blocks, no icons. ATS systems filter out
                 complex formatting. Standard fonts (Calibri, Arial),
                 consistent spacing, clear section headings.

Tone:            Professional and direct. Third person implied
                 (no "I" at the start of bullet points).
                 Use: action verb → task → quantified outcome.

========================================================================
SECTION ORDER (entry-level — follow this exact order)
========================================================================

1. Header
2. Professional Summary
3. Education
4. Work Experience
5. Technical Skills
6. Academic Projects
7. Volunteer and Extracurricular
8. Certifications (if relevant)
9. References

Note: Education appears ABOVE work experience for entry-level candidates.
Once a candidate has 2 or more years of professional experience, Education
moves below Work Experience. For this resume, keep Education above.

========================================================================
SECTION 1 — HEADER
========================================================================

Include ONLY:
  Full name (large, prominent)
  Suburb and state (e.g., Melbourne, VIC or Sydney, NSW) — no street address
  Phone number
  Email address
  LinkedIn URL
  GitHub URL (if applicable)
  Portfolio URL (if applicable)
  Working rights statement (REQUIRED — see below)

Do NOT include:
  Date of birth
  Marital status
  Nationality or country of origin
  Photo
  Street address or postcode

Working rights statement — include one of these as appropriate:
  Australian Citizen
  Australian Permanent Resident
  New Zealand Citizen
  Student Visa (Subclass 500) — full work rights
  Graduate Visa (Subclass 485) — full work rights
  Sponsored — Employer Nomination (Subclass 186)
  [Replace with the candidate's actual work rights status from the master resume]

This statement must appear in or directly below the header. Australian
recruiters screen on work rights before reading any other section.

========================================================================
SECTION 2 — PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY
========================================================================

3 to 4 sentences. For entry-level: frame around what you bring (skills,
study background, relevant experience) and what you are targeting
(type of role, industry, value you offer).

Do not write a generic objective. Reference the specific domain or type
of work the candidate is applying for.

Example structure:
  Sentence 1: Who you are and your qualification or study background.
  Sentence 2: What technical skills or tools you have built experience with.
  Sentence 3: What kind of role or environment you are targeting and why.
  Sentence 4 (optional): A notable achievement or differentiator.

========================================================================
SECTION 3 — EDUCATION
========================================================================

For each qualification, include:
  Degree name and major
  Institution name
  City and state
  Completion date (month and year, or expected)
  GPA or WAM (Weighted Average Mark) — include if above 3.5 GPA or 65+ WAM
  Relevant coursework (3 to 5 subjects most relevant to the target role)
  Academic achievements (Dean's List, scholarships, medals, honours)
  Thesis or capstone project title if applicable

Format:
  Institution name — bold
  Degree — regular text on the next line
  Dates — right-aligned or on the same line as the degree

========================================================================
SECTION 4 — WORK EXPERIENCE
========================================================================

Reverse chronological order (most recent first).
Include internships, part-time roles, casual roles, and contract work.
All work experience counts in the Australian market.

For each role, include:
  Job title
  Company name
  City and state (or Remote)
  Start date — End date (Month Year format, e.g., Jan 2025 — Aug 2025)
  6 to 7 bullet points per role

Bullet point format — use this pattern for every bullet:
  [Action verb] + [what you did or built] + [quantified outcome or scope]

Examples:
  Developed a Python ETL pipeline to consolidate 300+ Excel workbooks into
  a single master dataset, reducing manual reconciliation time by 4 hours weekly.

  Built a Tesseract OCR system to extract structured data from 300 unstructured
  shipping PDFs, replacing a manual process previously taking 3 days per cycle.

Rules for bullet points:
  Start with a strong past-tense action verb (Built, Developed, Designed,
  Implemented, Analysed, Automated, Created, Maintained, Supported, Tested).
  No first-person "I".
  Include a number, percentage, or scope indicator wherever possible.
  Each bullet one idea only. Maximum 25 words per bullet.
  4 to 5 bullets per role.

========================================================================
SECTION 5 — TECHNICAL SKILLS
========================================================================

Group skills into 5 categories:
  Programming and Engineering: [list tools and languages]
  AI and Machine Learning: [list frameworks and methods]
  Analytics and Visualisation: [list tools and techniques]
  Cloud, MLOps, and Deployment: [Infrastructure and model delivery]
  Data Architecture and Management: [Storage and pipeline systems]

Each category on its own line. Items separated by commas.
Each line ends with a full stop.
Maximum 6 to 8 items per line — keep it scannable.
Only include skills that appear in the master resume.
Order items by relevance to the target job description.

========================================================================
SECTION 6 — ACADEMIC PROJECTS
========================================================================

Especially important for entry-level candidates to compensate for limited
professional experience. Include 4 to 5 significant projects.

For each project:
  Project name — bold, with a one-line description of what it is
  4 to 5 bullet points using the same [action verb → task → outcome] pattern

Include:
  The problem the project addressed
  What you specifically built or contributed
  Technologies and methods used
  Scale or outcome (dataset size, accuracy, time saved, etc.)

========================================================================
SECTION 7 — VOLUNTEER AND EXTRACURRICULAR
========================================================================

This section is genuinely weighted by Australian recruiters — not filler.
Include any volunteer work, university clubs, student societies, mentoring,
open-source contributions, competition results, or community involvement.

For each entry:
  Role or activity name
  Organisation
  Dates
  1 to 2 bullet points on what you contributed or achieved

If there is nothing to include, omit this section entirely.

========================================================================
SECTION 8 — CERTIFICATIONS
========================================================================

Include only certifications directly relevant to the target role.
Maximum 6. Plain list, one per line, full stop at end of each line.

Format:
  Certification name — Issuing organisation — Year completed.

========================================================================
SECTION 9 — REFERENCES
========================================================================

For Australian resumes, one of two approaches is acceptable:

Option A (preferred if referees have given permission):
  List 2 referees with name, title, organisation, email, and phone.

Option B:
  "References available upon request."

Do not list referees who have not been asked for permission.

========================================================================
TYPOGRAPHY AND SPACING
========================================================================

Font:              Calibri or Arial — no decorative fonts
Heading size:      14pt bold for section headings
Subheading size:   12pt bold for role titles and project names
Body text:         11pt
Name (header):     18 to 20pt bold
Margins:           2cm (approximately 0.79 inches) all sides
Line spacing:      1.15 to 1.5 for body text — Australian resumes
                   have more breathing room than US one-pagers
Section spacing:   12pt space before each new section heading

========================================================================
COVER LETTER NOTE
========================================================================

A short cover letter (3 to 4 paragraphs, one page) is expected with
Australian applications — not optional. The cover letter should:
  Paragraph 1: What role you are applying for and why this company.
  Paragraph 2: What you bring that is directly relevant to this role.
  Paragraph 3: A specific example or achievement that demonstrates fit.
  Paragraph 4: Call to action — availability, contact, interview readiness.

The AI should note when generating a resume that a cover letter is
expected and should be submitted alongside the resume.
